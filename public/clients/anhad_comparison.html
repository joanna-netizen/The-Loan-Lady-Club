About Mortgage Insurance on Conventional Loans

Private mortgage insurance (PMI) is required on conventional loans when the down payment is less than 20%. The cost is primarily driven by your credit score and down payment percentage. At an estimated 760 FICO, the PMI shown above is relatively modest. Unlike FHA loans, PMI on a conventional loan can be removed once you reach 20% equity in the home.

About the 7/6 ARM (Physician Option C)

An adjustable-rate mortgage is fixed for the first 7 years and then adjusts every 6 months based on market conditions. Because you mentioned planning to stay 5 or more years, this is worth discussing carefully. The fixed period covers your full 3-year residency and several years beyond, but rate adjustments after year 7 introduce uncertainty. What the Data Shows

20% down is not required on a conventional loan. Conventional financing starts at 5% down. The tradeoff is a monthly mortgage insurance premium, which decreases as your down payment increases and disappears entirely at 20%.
The physician fixed-rate options carry a significant rate premium. Compared to conventional at 10% down, the physician fixed loans cost $454 to $563 more per month. Over 5 years that is roughly $27,000 to $33,800 in additional interest, which exceeds the MI savings at an estimated 760 FICO.
The physician 7/6 ARM closes the gap considerably. At 6.875%, the monthly payment of $4,984.95 is only $134 more than conventional at 10% down, with no MI. Whether the ARM makes sense depends on your plans after residency and your comfort with rate adjustment risk after year 7. This is a conversation worth having.
Your actual FICO score changes this picture. At an estimated 760, conventional PMI is relatively modest. If your score comes in lower, PMI costs rise and the rate advantage of conventional narrows. That is part of what we work through together before any decisions are made.
We do not yet know whether a co-signer is needed. Medical residents earn documented income through their residency program. Depending on your specific program and employment contract, you may qualify on your own. That is one of the first things we explore together.
