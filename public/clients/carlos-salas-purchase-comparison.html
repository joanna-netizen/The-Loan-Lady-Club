Understanding Your Two Rate Choices

Lower Rate vs. Premium Pricing

Within each purchase price, you have two interest rate options. The first is a lower rate with a small lender credit toward closing. The second is a higher rate that comes with a significantly larger lender credit. That second strategy is called premium pricing.

Here is how to think about the difference. When you take a lower rate, your monthly payment is smaller but you bring more cash to closing because the lender credit does not cover as much. When you take a higher rate through premium pricing, the lender pays a larger portion of your closing costs, which reduces what you bring to the table on closing day. The trade-off is a higher monthly payment for the life of the loan.

Neither choice is wrong. It depends on your current cash position and your long-term priorities. Both options are presented neutrally below so you can see the full picture and make the decision that fits your household.

How to Think About This

What Each Choice Really Means

1
The $10,000 difference in purchase price between the two properties adds approximately $56 to your monthly payment at the lower rate and approximately $59 at the higher rate. It also increases your down payment by $350. If both properties are otherwise comparable, the lower price gives you a slightly lower ongoing obligation.
2
The rate strategy is the bigger variable. Choosing the lower rate at 5.750% saves you approximately $124 per month on the $527,000 price and approximately $126 per month on the $537,000 price compared to the 6.125% option. Over 30 years, that difference is meaningful. The question is whether the savings in cash to close today is worth the higher payment over time.
3
The premium pricing options (1B and 2B) reduce your cash to close by approximately $3,700 to $3,800 compared to the lower rate options. If preserving cash at closing matters to your household right now, that lender credit is doing real work for you. If your cash position is comfortable, the lower rate is the better long-term value.
4
All four options include a 20-day prepaid interest calculation. That means closing is estimated to occur around the 10th or 11th of the month. The timing of your closing date affects this number, and it will be confirmed once we are under contract.
5
