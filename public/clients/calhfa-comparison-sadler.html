Understanding Mortgage Insurance

One of the most important differences between these two programs
Conventional: Private MI (PMI)
$90.53 / month

PMI on a conventional loan can be requested for removal once the loan balance reaches 80% of the original home value. It is automatically cancelled when the loan reaches 78% LTV based on the original amortization schedule. You are not paying this fee forever.

FHA: Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP)
$176.06 / month

FHA MIP includes both an upfront premium (financed into the loan at 1.75%) and a monthly premium. For most FHA loans with less than 10% down, the monthly MIP remains for the life of the loan. The most common way to remove FHA MIP is to refinance into a conventional loan once sufficient equity is built.

What This Means for Your Decision
Conventional Strength

Lower long-term insurance cost

The conventional PMI at $90.53 per month is significantly lower than the FHA MIP at $176.06. As you build equity, the PMI goes away. The FHA MIP is structured differently and stays on the loan unless you refinance. Over time, the conventional structure becomes less expensive on this line item.

FHA Strength

Lower cash to close today

FHA brings your cash to close to $5,798 compared to $6,722 on the conventional side. The additional MyHome assistance under FHA (3.5% cap vs 3%) covers the higher down payment requirement, and you arrive at closing with less out of pocket. If cash reserves are a priority right now, FHA has a structural advantage.

FHA Strength

Lower minimum credit score

FHA allows a minimum qualifying score of 640 compared to 680 for the conventional program. If either borrower's qualifying score is below 680, the FHA path may be the more accessible option without adjusting the loan structure.

Conventional Strength

Lower note rate on this scenario

The conventional note rate shown is 7.000%. The FHA note rate shown is 6.750%. While FHA has a lower note rate here, the APR tells a fuller story: the FHA APR is 7.716% vs the conventional APR of 7.406%. The financed upfront MIP increases the effective cost of borrowing under FHA, which the APR reflects.

Both Programs

ZIP and MyHome are deferred, not forgiven

Both programs use CalHFA's Zero Interest Program (ZIP) and MyHome Assistance. These are real liens recorded on your property. They carry no interest and no monthly payments, but repayment is due when you sell, refinance, or pay off the first mortgage. This is not free money. It is structured assistance that defers cost to a future transaction.

Both Programs

Homebuyer education is required

CalHFA requires completion of an approved homebuyer education course before closing. Certificates are valid for one year. This applies to all first-time homebuyers using either the conventional or FHA CalHFA program with subordinate financing. Planning ahead on this requirement avoids delays at the end of the process.

There is no universally better option here. The right program depends on your credit profile, your cash on hand, and your plan for the home over the next several years. My job is to make sure you understand exactly what each structure means before we decide together.
