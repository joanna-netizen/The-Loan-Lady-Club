What This Means for You

Every option uses the same 3% down payment of $7,200. The difference is how the interest rate is priced.

A higher rate brings in more lender credit, which covers more of your closing costs. That is why 7.250% needs the least cash today, about $7,694.

A lower rate means less lender credit, so more of the closing costs land on you. That is why 6.875% needs the most cash today, about $9,986, even though the rate itself is lower.

In plain terms: 7.250% keeps the most cash in your pocket today. 7.000% gives you the lowest monthly payment. 6.875% has the lowest rate, but it asks for the most cash upfront.

Example only: the exact cash needed and payment can shift slightly once your loan is locked and your final costs are confirmed.
